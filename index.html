Design as a Conversation

By framing the design process as a conversation, you improve your team's understanding of each milestone and what the overall priorities should be. Everyone is engaged, encouraged to ask questions, and able to add feedback or suggestions without disrupting the process.

Laying out the Map

Design documentation is rigid and difficult to scale. By laying out end-user goals, activities, and tasks in a visually intuitive and malleable way, you're able to display and explore ideas more quickly and completely, without losing sight of the big picture or your project deadlines.

An Iterative Process

Designing complete modules and putting them together makes implementing user feedback more difficult and less efficient. By creating a skeleton, or minimum viable product, you can understand what bottlenecks and pain points are keeping your design from being the best it can be.

Tell the Story of a User's Path Through Your Project

Story mapping is the process of designing an application by following a user's path through the various tasks they need to complete in order to meet their goals. Rather than starting off your development by assuming you know what the finished product will look like, story mapping requires you to remain fluid and adapt to change, working in iterations rather than increments. This means building a functional product early on and then working to improve it as you recieve feedback, instead of creating "finished" version of different pieces, one after the other.
